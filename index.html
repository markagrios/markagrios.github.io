Design of this website

This website is the product of my fascination of both aesthetics and function. My interest in simple design, typography, and contrast has lead the design of this website to the place it is now. The animations and layout utilize certain principles of psychology and mathematics to make the design appealing. See if you can find the golden ratio in the spacing of certain elements ;D. Also, you might notice how most of the animations I made have small changes in opacity somewhere in them which plays on the brain's response to something seeming to appear rather than something changing that was already there.

Why do people hate math?

Mathematics seems to be the only subject that is socially acceptable to openly deplore. I understand that some people might not be very good at it or don't understand it fully or just aren't interested. However, I believe what we (those who enjoy math) need to make known to people is that the world of mathematics is a beautiful and wonderul place not bounded by rules and formulas like what most people think. Scientists will say math is a tool while mathematicians say it is a science and an art by itself. Both labels are equally valid but that does not stop a large proportion of people from despising it in practice. People see that great achievements of scientists and artists and wonder how amazing they are but a minute amount of people have ever wondered what tools the Wright brothers used to build their flying machine or what paintbrush Michelangelo used to paint the Sistine Chapel.

From what I have seen, math is taught in a seemingly backwards style. Most subjects are taught and, to make them relatable, some background is given to let the students understand why it is important. In some way, foreground must be given to students taking math in order for it to seem relevant. Students are taught the different ways and approaches to solving problems but are almost never shown what these theorems and principles can lead to down the road. Displaying pi as an infinite rational expression or extrapolating the factorial function to all real numbers are a beautiful example of pure mathematics in practice.

Thinking of a moment where you used the math you learned in first grade is not hard. Trying to find a time you needed to factor a seventh degree polynomial for some reason is a bit trickier. Math builds on itself, not that other subjects do not, but most people find that the fundamentals of math are simply the only ones they need while most other areas, the most advanced and complex disciplines are the most appealing, think music and art. This, I believe, is the pitfall that many people fall into. You go through school learning more and more about subjects that interest you and you get to understand why they are important to the world and why you personally might find them fascinating. Mathematics seems to lose many people because it gets arbitrarily complicated as it progresses fading away from people's lists of "relevant" topics. If nothing else, I want people to look at mathematics the same way they look at other fields of art and science. To appreciate it as its own field of study that has everything music and science and language and everything has, not this alien clutter of obnoxiously cryptic symbols that explain irrelevant things I'll never use in life. So, whether you like mathematics or not, take a moment to look into it next time you come across it and try to find its simple elegance in logic that is so applicable to the real world.

Projects

This is where I'll post my projects, as sparse and unfinished as they might be, you can get an idea as to what I'm interested in.

Map Generator (press 'R' to load a new map)

This is a random map generator I made for a game my friend and I were working with but never took off. It randomly assigns certain cells to different terrains to start with and then uses a recursive algorithm to expand outward from there. I tried to model its behavior after real techtonic plate movements so I attempted to add some pseudo-entropy at the expense of run time.

DICE

Digital Correction Engine, or DICE, is a current project of mine to explore image and data analysis with correction algorithms. Right now it can't do much but in the future it should be able to straighten lines and smooth out curves you draw with your mouse.
