About me.

As a student from Washington D.C. I've found numerous places to fuel my never ending curiosity from building robots to performing magic on the streets.

I love building robots. I've been involved with my high school's robotics team being co-captain three years and president my senior year. We compete in Botball and FTC and have won awards and honors for my code using color camera interpolation and infrared orientation. I have also been a member of my school TSA (Technology Students Association) chapter for four years and was vice-president my junior year. We won second place for the animatronics event for the state of Virginia my first two years. My friends and I also compete in ctf's (capture the flag) which are information security competitions where students compete to find keys and break codes. Most of my contributions to the team involve the math behind cryptography in order to break keys or restore lost information, but I can get away with a few networking challenges here and there.

As fascinated as I am with computers and science and math, I am also a performing magician and hypnotist. I have always loved interacting with people and having a way of doing that where I also entertain as well as learn makes magic something I enjoy very much. I started with a deck of cards when I was in seventh grade and have since then moved on to other fields of magic such as mentalism and large stage illusions and have even created and published some of my own.
