About —

Hi I'm Mark.

As a student from Washington DC, I've found plenty of ways to fuel my infinite curiosity. From graphic design to building robots to performing magic on the streets, I love learning and doing things that make me think.

My first fascination I became truly engrossed in was computer programming which I'm still very active in. I coded infrared navigation and color camera interpolation for my highschool robotics team and continue to have various projects going on (check out my projects). I picked up web designing my senior year of highschool and made this website to battle the never ending struggle of aesthetics and function.

I'm a math and science dork (I'll never be able to escape that, even if I wanted to) but I run my little design business and read just as many F. Scott Fitzgerald books as I do journals on math and biology. My very first job was being a designer at a small tech company where I remade their website and worked with an actual designer to give the company a consistent visual appearence to their clients (I got to pick the company's official font). I am now cursed with having to walk around everywhere making note of the fonts on every street sign, menu, and flyer I come across.

Overall, I just love learning things. I can incorporate the math and psychology I learn in class into the designs I make for people and can relate my fascination with words and color to the research I do at school. The world is too big for me to pick "science" or "art" so I decided to play with both.

Current Research —

I'm very lucky to be doing research as an undergraduate. I'm working with two professors to study biological neural networks using persistent homology. I'm using Brian simulator (yes Brian not Brain), a neuron spiking simulator package for Python, to build a Hindmarsh-Rose model for a small neural network that we can then look at using techniques from algebraic topolgy.

PushBuddy —

My freshman year of college, my friends and I competed in VT Hacks III and won second place for our hack. We wanted to tackle the confusion and frustration that comes with all the different cloud storage services out there and PushBuddy was born. I designed the website :D

LeToLi —

Learn To Live is a rather large project I'm working on. My fascination with neuroscience and mathematics has lead me to start constructing a learning neural network. I built a simulation world and put and organism in it that knows how to move, eat, drink, and sleep and has different stats for each. Once I'm done I'll put it on its own website where anyone can check in on him to watch him live, die, and learn from his mistakes.

I'm using mean curvature flow over smooth topologies as an optimizing mechanic and continuous threshold biased neurons. I'm trying to model LeToLi's learning after a single organisms brain solving local problems not genetic algorithms that model the adaptations of a species like other machine learning programs.
