People's distaste with Mathematics.

Why do some people hate math?

Most people actually don't hate mathematics because they do not know exactly what mathematics is or rather what it can be. People are taught in school about how rote and concrete math is and you cannot stray from the rules. I have to say that this is untrue. What people learn in school is applied mathematics, it is the model of how this and function in the world we live in when the entirety of mathematics truly much large and much more beautiful.

The biggest obstacle that people face with math is mistaking its function for its purpose. The true function of math that we see is to explain the world around us in a concrete way. Its purpose is actually much more magnificent because of its simplicity. Mathematics is its own entity, it is a formal system of which certain truths are accepted and the rest is all interpretation from there. The reason mathematics is taught is for its function, it is a tool. The thing people don't appreciate about mathematics is that they see it as only a tool. There has never been a person who looked at the Wright brothers' flying machine and glorified the tools used to build it, nor looked at Picasso's art and pay any attention to the paintbrushes he used.So when someone says the hate mathematics they are really saying that they have failed to understand the potential of the tools they were given.

What could one stop "hating" math?

This problem should be addressed really in two ways. There are people that dislike the idea of all the rules and do not find value in the function and purpose of math and other are simply just "bad" at math and that is why they hate it. "Bad" is not the best word to be used here, many people how are "bad" at math simply had trouble grasping the abstract concepts of what they were taught or just enjoyed other subject more in their schooling career. Again, math is a tool and that function is taught at an early age. Children first learn to count and then add and then multiply etc. They people who dislike math, most likely, never stepped past the question of "what more can math do besides explain what's around me?" That is a perfectly rational notion to entertain, when given an object, oh say, an iPhone, people new to this object often have the question of "what else can it do?" Mathematics is the same way, people who do not follow their natural curiosity of pursuing something's potential, no matter how abstract or concrete it may be, are often the ones who end up with a distaste for mathematics.

What kind of people are mathematicians?

Many people find mathematicians and scientists the most concrete and unimaginative people. This does have some truth in it, but, the people who find no interest in math and science are often very rational. They didn't look at the line 1+1=2 and ask why, they accepted it for what the world agreed on and continued. This is not at all a bad thing, people have different interests and ideas. The idea of mathematics beyond what most people see it as is a very amorphous concept and generally people who find interest in the real world seem to sway in the opposite direction as math and more towards science, music, art, literature, because they find more value in the finished product rather than the tools.
