About me.

Hey! I'm Mark! I'm a senior in high school interested in everything I don't already know. Most of my curiousity lies within the realm of sciences and math rather than the arts but I love it all just the same. I've been involved in my high school's TSA (Technology Students Association) chapter for four years and was vice president my junior year. I have also spent my four years of high school on the robotics team being co-captain the last three years. In middle school at around the time I learned to ride a unicycle, I picked up the art of performing magic. Since then I have explored and created many of my own illusions and have performed on the streets of Washington D.C. doing tricks with everyday items and hypnotizing spectators. I've always been interested in music. I appreciate the structure and complexities that lie within a piece of music and I like taking apart movements and see what makes certain parts so enjoyable. The only visual art media I am somewhat involved in is photography. I enjoy exploring with light and contrast so black and white prints (especially of nature) I often find intriguing. However, more than music and photography, I find language the most amazing. With my practice in the art of hypnotism, I have come to appreciate the small facets of meaning we can put into spoken language. The deep ambiguity of speech only seems ambiguous because of the lack of conscious thought we put into it. I find this truly astounding.

What I do.

Quite a lot, I believe. My curiosity with information has led to the robots we used in our competitions to sort through different colored balls using camera interpolation. Most of what I do in the field of computers focuses on certain concepts such as color identification, infinite sequences, or the random map generator on cabrera.io (in progress) as opposed to practically developed computer applications. My fascination with music lead to me teaching myself how to read music and basic music theory after I already taught myself to play the basics on piano, guitar, bass, and ukulele. I'm currently in the process of learning Korean which is very nice to take a break from the western cyrillic alphabet. It is interesting to see what vowels and consonants different spoken languages have that others do not. My love for things dexterous and physics makes my unicycle-riding more fun than it should be. It is hard for me even to comprehend what and how my brain communicates with my muscles as I balance on a single point on a plane with most of the weight relatively high. It's the mix of brain functions and physics and skill that makes not only riding a unicycle fun but also thinking about it. This website is an effort to mix functionality with aesthetics which is one of the more enjoyable wars I've fought in my lifelong journey. I believe most of my inspiration for my design and layout comes from my love for the color red (darkish deep red specifically) and subtle class found in simple but not minimal design. If you're wondering, the hex value of this red I use is #870B0B.
